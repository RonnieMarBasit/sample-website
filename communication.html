DISCIPLINE OF COMMUNICATION

HOME SOCIAL WORK COUNSELING COMMUNICATION

The discipline of communication refers to the academic field of study that explores the processes, theories, and practices related to human communication. It encompasses a wide range of topics and approaches, including interpersonal communication, mass communication, organizational communication, intercultural communication, and more.

COMMUNICATION

Communication is the process of exchanging information, ideas, thoughts, and feelings between individuals or groups. It is a fundamental aspect of human interaction and plays a crucial role in conveying messages and establishing understanding between people.

CLIENTILE AND AUDIENCES IN COMMUNICATION

AUDIENCES OF COMMUNICATION
In communication, the public is the general concept that pertains to the recipient of the message in an interpersonal, group, organizational and government setting.



Communication audiences may be categorized as:

Individuals

In the discipline of communication, individuals refer to single persons who are the recipients or participants in the communication process. Understanding the characteristics, needs, and preferences of individuals is crucial in designing effective communication strategies and messages that resonate with them.


Communities

Communities in communication refer to groups of individuals who share common interests, values, or geographical locations. Communication within communities involves understanding their collective identity, norms, and communication patterns, as well as addressing their specific needs and concerns.


Groups and Organization

Groups and organizations in communication refer to formal or informal associations of individuals with shared goals or purposes. Communication within groups and organizations focuses on facilitating effective collaboration, coordination, and information exchange among members to achieve common objectives.


Media Audiences

Media audiences are the individuals or groups who consume various forms of media, including television, radio, newspapers, magazines, websites, and social media platforms. Understanding media audiences helps in tailoring content, selecting appropriate channels, and engaging with the intended recipients in an effective and targeted manner.


Mass vs. Niche

Mass and niche audiences refer to different scopes of audience reach in communication. Mass audiences are large, diverse groups of people who are exposed to widely distributed media content or messages. Niche audiences, on the other hand, are smaller, specialized groups with specific interests, often targeted through more focused or specialized media channels or content.


ONLINE AUDIENCE

The online audience specifically refers to individuals or groups who consume or engage with content through digital platforms and channels, such as websites, social media, blogs, podcasts, or online forums. Understanding the online audience involves considering their digital behaviors, preferences, and engagement patterns.


AUDIENCE ANALYSIS AND AUDIENCE RESEARCH

Audience analysis and audience research involve studying and understanding the characteristics, attitudes, behaviors, and preferences of specific audiences. It includes methods such as surveys, focus groups, interviews, and data analysis to gather insights that inform communication strategies, message development, and channel selection.


COMMUNICATION AND MEDIA CHANNELS

Communication and media channels refer to the various means through which messages are transmitted or conveyed to audiences. These can include print media, broadcast media, digital platforms, interpersonal communication, public speaking, advertising, social media, and more. Choosing the appropriate communication and media channels is essential for effectively reaching and engaging specific audiences based on their characteristics, preferences, and media consumption habits.



Mass media

Mass media, which include broadcast (radio and television) and print remain the most popular communication way to reach large audiences due to its availability and wide coverage. Briefly, let us look at all these forms of mass media and gain knowledge when to use them to attain our communication goals.


Print

Print media include newspapers, magazines, brochures, pamphlets, catalogues, journals, and such like. There has been a decrease with the use of print media, mainly newspapers, in recent times due to the popularity of access to news as well as other information on mobile devices and online remains. Print media remain important to the dissemination of information that serves as a resource artifact of history. It is a quantifiable and easily accessible source of historical information, and that is something we purchase and own, as opposed to television and radio programs


Radio

Even before TV collected the imaginations of the Filipinos; radio was yet another source of information and entertainment. Because of its lower cost compared to television, radio is used for both commercial and developmental purposes, particularly in rural communities. Even in modern times, with the threat of calamities, radio has once again proved its worth in delivering information directly, as compared to television. Radio can also be enjoyed everywhere and has achieved a higher level of loyalty to the viewers who will always be tuned in to their programs.


Television

The advantage of television compared with the other two forms of media is its audio-visual capacity. It is designed, by its natural world, to attract the attention of the audience for just a short period of time, which means that the messages destined for this medium should be catchy, convincing, and attentive. In today's modern media landscape, reality TV is becoming the main feature for news, entertainment, and education; its influence has also been explained, debated, and even studied by scholars.


New Media

As major aspect of the telecommunications network, the Internet is perhaps the most essential part of the convergence puzzle. It is a vehicle that can incorporate all the other forms of media. Moreover, the feedback capability of Internet is considered as one of its most attractive and essential features. Although there are many issues concerning the use of Internet in terms of impact, abuse, privacy, credibility, and accessibility, it cannot be denied that the communications industry, as well as its audience, has grown radically due to the Internet


Social media

Social media is a subset of latest media that allow the creation and exchange of content. The creation of highly interactive devices from which individuals and communities’ access, co-create, discuss, and revise information depends on mobile and web-based technologies. Social media sites are used to connect with friends and family members (Facebook and Twitter), to generate and post their own content (YouTube, Instagram, Sound Cloud), to share photos and videos (Vimeo, Vine, Pinterest) and even to share highly qualified affiliations and links (LinkedIn).
